<h2 id="functional-prototype">Functional Prototype</h2>

<h3>Purpose</h3>
<p>
The purpose of our functional prototype was to validate our design across key requirements, including paddle rotation and the durability of the physical structure. In addition, another objective was to validate our manufacturing processes and concepts once implemented. The physical prototype provided an effective medium to test new ideas, iterate, and identify improvements for our final design.
</p>

<h3>What Was Tested</h3>
<p>
The key tests we conducted validated the prototype’s motion, interference, fastening and stability, and performance under repeated use/cycling. These tests were generally performed under consistent conditions—rotation at 60 rpm for 1 minute—while recording observations and measurements relevant to each case. For example, when testing fastening and stability, we evaluated visible flex or shifting in the structure and how securely the shaft remained seated throughout operation.</p>

<h3>Outcome</h3>
<p>
Overall, the tests were successful across the most critical criteria, including smooth rotation, effective surface skimming, and appropriate scaling. However, we observed some unintended motion beyond the primary rotation, which will require retolerancing and a redesign of the support structures in the next iteration.</p>
